I was motivated, so spent most of my Saturday doing the case manufacturing and assembly.

I began with a mock assembly with painter's tape

Next, I added the inside cover. I had cut out a port for a passport cover, traced around a popsicle stick template. As the inside cover needed to accommodate the cover of the passport, I only want to glue around the outside edge, not the full surface.

Next, I glued down the eBook grips, and once that had set, used an edger and stitching chisels to form the stitching holes.

Then I cut and stitched the inset cover, as well as the grips. I also reinforced one side of the passport slot.

I also cut a port on the back to allow access to the power button on the back of the Kobo. I probably should have done that before I glued on the grips. Whoops.

As a final addition, I added a pen loop to the snap closure, reinforced the closure, and added the snap.

However, when I had it all put together with the pen, there wasn't quite enough space to actually have the pen in the loop and still snap it shut. I may add a second pen loop to the spine between the passport and eBook.

Alternately, I'll remake the closure and give the pen more space. Drawbacks there is that the closure wouldn't be seamless, but I could make it decorative.

Or both. It might be better to remove the almost-useless pen loop. I'll give it some thought...
