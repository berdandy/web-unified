My wife and I both got Kobo eBooks for ourselves, but she was unhappy with the stock case options. When I got into leatherworking, I offered to make her a custom one once I had figured out how to not make terrible things.

There are a few options I explored:

  • Slipcover. Decided against, because it's not a permanent case.
  • Corner-grips. Decided aginst, because one corner would block the charging port.
  • Velcro-mount for a floating effect. Decided against, aesthetic reasons.
  • Wet-formed grips. This seems the most workable option.

After some digging, I finally found a random photo of someone who had done a similar thing, so I was confident I wasn't completely off the mark. I wet-formed top-and-bottom "grips" for a test, and rough-cut material for a cover.

I can picture it now.

The remaining plan is:

  1. Cut a "reversed" cover pocket to accommodate a passport. A strong secondary use for this case is a passport holder.
  2. Cut a pen holder to sit in snap loop (or spine?)
  3. Assemble with temporary tape
  4. Cut cover to shape, including snap loop
  5. Finish raw leather grips (cover and pocket material is already-finished leather)
  6. Finish all interior edges
  7. Assemble with grips and cover pocket and final trim
  8. Finish exterior edges
  9. Attach snap closure
